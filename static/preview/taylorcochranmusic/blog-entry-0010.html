I used to think pursuing a passion was scary. Now I know I'm right.

05.08.2014

MORE BLOGS

After a year of thinking, praying, and waiting I decided to leave my corporate job of money, benefits, and security to pursue a lifestyle that involves risk, almost certain failure, and unknown direction. What a tradeoff, right?

 

Thanks to mentorships and good friends, I had the opportunity to record my debut EP as a singer/songwriter with a producer based out of Nashville, TN. Most musicians I’ve met seem to have come out of the womb with a guitar knowing they want to make a career in the music industry. They seem so confident in their career choice. As for me, music has been a way to serve people by sending a positive message. One gig lead to another, and sure enough, I am caught at the crossroads unemployed with mini-Taylor pacing around in my head thinking about jobs, where I should live, how I should pursue this career, you name it. Needless to say, I’m overwhelmed.

 

Sitting on some pollen coated patio furniture, I began sharing my hesitation with a friend. He told me of a time in his life when he was desperately seeking security in the midst of his career path in the music industry. Despite his frantic search for money and work, God gave him one answer: patience. I’m pretty sure my buddy would’ve preferred God give him a record deal or a leprechaun or something a little more useful, but something tells me God knew better than that.

 

Every morning for a couple weeks, instead of heading straight to jobs.com or the classified ads, my friend would reluctantly sit on his patio with his Bible. Sometimes he would read it, and sometimes he wouldn’t. Sometimes he’d pray, and sometimes he’d just sit, waiting. After two weeks of doing this (mind you, this must have felt like an eternity when you have a mortgage to pay), his resilience gradually turned to submission. He would wake up every morning and ask the most refreshing words I’ve heard in a while, “God, what do you want to do today?”

That my friends, is how we are meant to live. When the desires of our heart are not in ourselves, but in something greater, we’ll be in a good place.

 

There is a story in the Bible of this guy named Jesus (maybe you’ve heard of him) who goes on top of a mountain and preaches to a group of people. Jesus speaks words I think were meant directly for me. He says, “Look at the birds of the air: they neither so nor reap nor gather into barns, and yet your heavenly Father feeds them. Are you not of more value than they?…Therefore do not be anxious saying, ‘What shall we eat?’ or ‘What shall we drink?’ or ‘What shall we wear?’… Your heavenly Father knows that you need them all. But seek first the Kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things will be added to you” (Matthew 6:26 – 34).

 

We have a God that delights in being with us and providing for us. My challenge for you in the next week is to stop and breathe. Stop thinking about where you’ll be, the next best job, or hot date you want to go on and simply wake up in the morning like my buddy did and say, “God, what do you want to do today?” I have a hunch that he knows what he’s doing.
